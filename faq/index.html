Orbital logistics is not just about moving payloads. It is the coordination infrastructure that could unlock entirely new economies in Earth orbit, cislunar space, and beyond.

About Aether

Aether is the coordination layer for space transportation. We build the software infrastructure that unifies mission planning, launch allocation, and manifest optimisation — replacing fragmented spreadsheet-driven workflows with a programmable, high-utilisation platform for the orbital economy.

Aether serves launch providers, satellite operators, payload integrators, and mission planners. If your work involves getting hardware to orbit — or coordinating between those who do — Aether is built for you.

We are headquartered in London, with a distributed team operating remotely across time zones. We work closely with partners in the UK, Europe, and North America.

The Platform

The space industry is growing rapidly, but mission planning and launch coordination still rely on disconnected tools — spreadsheets, emails, and manual processes. This creates scheduling inefficiencies, manifest errors, and wasted capacity. Aether replaces this with a single programmable system that brings real-time visibility and optimisation to the entire launch stack.

Aether ingests mission requirements from operators and launch providers, then applies scheduling algorithms and orbital mechanics to generate optimised manifests and transfer windows. The platform surfaces this as a unified dashboard and programmable API — giving all stakeholders a shared operational picture.

Yes. Aether incorporates orbital mechanics at the core of mission planning — including Hohmann transfer window modelling, trajectory design, and Kepler-correct scheduling. This allows the platform to reason about feasibility and efficiency across missions, not just logistics.

Aether is designed as a programmable platform — accessible via dashboard for operators and via API for technical integrations. We work closely with early partners to configure the platform for their specific workflows during this phase of development.
