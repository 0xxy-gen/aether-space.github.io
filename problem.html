01 / Fragmented Procurement

Launch slots are sourced through a patchwork of brokers, direct outreach, and RFI processes. There is no single interface to compare, allocate, or optimise across providers.

02 / Manual Mission Planning

Trajectory design, manifest optimisation, and transfer window scheduling are done manually or with siloed tools. Errors propagate downstream into costly delays and mass penalties.

03 / No Coordination Layer

As cislunar traffic grows, multi-stakeholder missions — shared rideshares, lunar relay chains, orbital depots — have no common operating picture. Coordination happens over email.

04 / Utilisation Waste

Launch vehicles routinely fly with unused mass margin because payloads cannot be efficiently matched, stacked, or rescheduled in real time. Capacity is left on the pad.

05 / Scaling Wall

The processes that work at 50 launches per year will collapse at 500. The cislunar economy requires infrastructure-grade logistics software before it can reach full operational tempo.
